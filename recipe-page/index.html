Ingredients

  • 3 pounds beef chuck or other boneless stewing beef, cut into 2-inch cubes and patted dry
  • 2 ¼ teaspoons kosher salt
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 5 ounces pancetta or bacon, diced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 large carrot, sliced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 750 ml bottle of red wine
  • 1 large bay leaf
  • 1 large sprig of thyme
  • 8 ounces pearl onions, peeled
  • 8 ounces cremini mushrooms
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Pinch of sugar

Preparation

  1. Season beef with 2 teaspoons salt and 1/2 teaspoon pepper. Set aside for at least 30 minutes at room temperature, or chill in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours.
  2. In a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot with a tightfitting lid, cook bacon over medium-low heat until fat is rendered and bacon is browned and crisp, about 10 to 15 minutes. Transfer with a slotted spoon to a paper towel-lined plate. Reserve fat in pot.
  3. Heat oven to 350 degrees fahrenheight. Raise heat under pot to medium-high and cook until fat starts to smoke. Lay half the beef cubes in a single layer in the pot, leaving space between pieces. Cook until well browned on all sides, 10 to 15 minutes; transfer pieces to a plate as they brown. Repeat with remaining beef.
  4. Reduce heat. Stir in onion, carrot and remaining 1/4 teaspoon salt and cook until soft, about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Stir in garlic and tomato paste, and cook for 1 minute.
  6. Stir in flour, cook for 1 minute, then add wine, bay leaf and thyme, scraping up brown bits at bottom of pot.
  7. Add browned beef and half the cooked bacon to pot, cover, and transfer to oven. Let cook until beef is very tender, about 1 1/2 hours, turning meat halfway through.
  8. While the pot is in the oven, in a large skillet set over high heat, combine pearl onions, mushrooms, 1/4 cup water, olive oil and a pinch each of salt, pepper and sugar. Bring to a simmer, then cover and reduce heat to medium, cooking for 15 minutes.
  9. Uncover, raise heat to high, and cook, tossing frequently, until vegetables are well browned, 5 to 7 minutes.
  10. To serve, scatter onions and mushrooms and remaining cooked bacon over stew.
